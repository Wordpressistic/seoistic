# Internal Linking Guidance

Internal links distribute topical authority ("link equity") across a site and help both users and crawlers understand the relationship between pages. They are one of the few SEO levers fully within a site owner's control.

## What makes a good internal link suggestion
- The anchor text should describe the destination topic specifically — not "click here" or "read more".
- Link to genuinely related content: a deeper resource on a sub-topic mentioned in passing, a relevant category/hub page, or a page that logically follows from what the reader just read.
- Prioritize linking to important, underlinked pages (cornerstone content, key service/product pages, pages with few existing internal links) over pages that are already heavily linked.
- 2–5 contextual internal links per typical content page is a reasonable range — too few wastes the opportunity, too many dilutes relevance and looks manipulative.

## Anchor text guidance
- Vary anchor text naturally across different links to the same page — do not repeat the exact same over-optimized phrase every time.
- Keep anchor text concise (2–5 words) and grammatically natural within the sentence it sits in.

## What this generator should output
Since the assistant does not have a live index of every URL on the site, it should describe *what topic or page type* to link to and suggested anchor text — not invent a specific URL. The site owner or the internal-link tool matches that description to a real destination.

## Anti-patterns to avoid recommending
- Linking every occurrence of a keyword throughout the page to the same target.
- Adding links purely for volume without topical relevance.
- Recommending links to unrelated high-authority pages just to "spread" link equity — internal links should reflect real topical relationships.
