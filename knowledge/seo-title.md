# SEO Title & Meta Description Guidance

## SEO title rules
- Target 50–60 characters — Google truncates around 600px, roughly 60 characters for most Latin scripts.
- Put the primary focus keyword as close to the start as possible without sounding forced.
- One idea per title. Do not stuff multiple keywords separated by pipes or commas.
- Write for a human clicking a search result, not for an algorithm — it must read as a normal, compelling sentence fragment.
- Avoid ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation ("!!!", "???"), and clickbait that the page content doesn't deliver on.
- Include the brand/site name only if there is room after the primary keyword phrase; never let the brand name push out the keyword.
- Never wrap the title in quotation marks — the field is the whole title, not a quoted phrase.

## Meta description rules
- Target 140–160 characters. Under 120 wastes the available snippet space; over 160 gets truncated with an ellipsis.
- Include the focus keyword once, naturally — it gets bolded in search results when it matches the query.
- End with a concrete reason to click: what the reader gets, learns, or solves — not a vague teaser.
- Write in active voice, second person where natural ("Learn how...", "Get..."), and avoid restating the title verbatim.
- Do not make claims the page content does not support (dates, prices, guarantees) — this is a live commitment to the reader.

## Focus keyword rules
- A focus keyword is the primary phrase this page should rank for — usually 2–4 words, matching real search intent, not a single generic word.
- Prefer specific, intent-matching phrases over broad/generic ones (e.g. "best waterproof hiking boots" over "boots").
- The keyword should appear naturally in the title, the first 100 words of content, and at least one heading — never forced or repeated unnaturally (no keyword stuffing).
